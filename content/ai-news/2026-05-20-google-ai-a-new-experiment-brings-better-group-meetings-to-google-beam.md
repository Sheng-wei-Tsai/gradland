## What was announced

Google Beam expands group meeting capabilities with a new experiment that renders remote participants at true-to-life size on HP Dimension immersive displays, as if they're sitting around a physical table. Each participant's voice is anchored via spatial audio directly to their position. The feature works automatically across devices—home or office—and integrates with Google Workspace and Zoom.

## Why it matters

If you're building real-time collaboration or video conferencing tools, this is a reference implementation for closing the 'hybrid inclusion gap.' The research is concrete: 50% stronger sense of social connection and 21% improvement in participants' ability to contribute. For developers on the Google Workspace/Beam ecosystem, this signals where Google is investing; spatial audio + life-size rendering reduce fatigue and disengagement that standard grid-based UIs create. If you're architecting remote-first products, these metrics justify the investment in better AV infrastructure.
